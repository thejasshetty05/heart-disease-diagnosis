Heart Disease Diagnosis

About Heart Disease

Heart disease refers to various conditions affecting the heart, such as coronary artery disease, arrhythmias, or congenital defects. Maintaining a healthy lifestyle can significantly reduce your risk.

Tips for a Healthy Heart

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
  • Exercise regularly and maintain a healthy weight.
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption.
  • Manage stress effectively through relaxation techniques.

Medical Information About Inputs

  • Age: Represents the age of the individual, as age is a significant risk factor for heart disease.
  • Sex: Biological sex influences the risk and presentation of heart disease.
  • Chest Pain Type: Categorizes the type of chest pain, which can indicate various underlying heart conditions. For instance, angina often signifies restricted blood flow to the heart.
  • Resting Blood Pressure: High resting blood pressure (hypertension) can increase the risk of heart disease and complications.
  • Cholesterol: High levels of cholesterol, especially LDL, are associated with an increased risk of plaque buildup in arteries.
  • Fasting Blood Sugar: Elevated fasting blood sugar levels may indicate diabetes, a condition that significantly raises heart disease risk.
  • Resting ECG Results: An electrocardiogram (ECG) can detect heart abnormalities such as arrhythmias, ischemia, or hypertrophy.
  • Maximum Heart Rate Achieved: Indicates the heart's efficiency and condition during physical exertion.
  • Exercise Induced Angina: Refers to chest pain triggered by exercise, often linked to inadequate oxygen supply to the heart.
  • ST Depression: Changes in the ST segment on an ECG can signal ischemia or other cardiac issues.
  • Slope of ST Segment: The slope provides insight into the heart's response to exercise and can indicate ischemia severity.
  • Number of Major Vessels: Fluoroscopic imaging helps identify blockages in the major coronary arteries.
